Background

The Centre for Reading and Language (CRL) has expertise childrenâ€™s development of language, reading and number skills and uses this knowledge to investigate disorders of language and cognition including dyslexia, language impairment, speech sound disorder, dyscalculia, attention problems, autism spectrum disorders and learning disabilities such as Down syndrome and 22q. Working as a freelancer, I designed and developed the website for the centre.

Brief

Develop a website with the purpose of providing potential patients with information about the services the CRL provide and keep academic staff and stakeholders informed of the latest news and research.

Goals and Objectives

  • Accessible
  • Usable on mobile devices
  • Ease of use for editors to update news

Research

Through requirement gathering and stakeholder interviews I established that the website's target audiences. Academics that would want to know about the latest news and parents/guardians that would be looking for more information about the CRL before sending their children. I was also informed that many of the website's users would potentially be dyslexic users.

I worked with Professor Snowling at CRL back in 2012 to create a web presence for the team that would be accessible for users, be responsive and be easy for them to update.

Design

I designed the site in Photoshop and through speaking with Professor Snowling I found a lot out about what she was hoping for from the website. The beige colour that the content sits on was chosen as it can be easier for users with dyslexia to read from. I used a pattern from Subtle Patterns for the header and footer and designed the website in Photoshop. I love to speak directly with clients.
